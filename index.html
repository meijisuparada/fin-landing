Where this data comes from
Federal tax brackets — IRS Rev. Proc. 2023-34 (2024 tax year)
Standard deduction — IRS Publication 501 ($14,600 single / $29,200 MFJ for 2024)
FICA exemption for F1/OPT — IRS Publication 519, Chapter 8. Students on F-1 visas in "nonresident alien" status are exempt from Social Security & Medicare taxes.
Social Security wage base — SSA Notice 2023-75 ($168,600 for 2024)
State tax rates — each state's Department of Revenue (2024 published rates, middle bracket estimates)
Limitations — This is an estimate. It does not account for additional Medicare tax (0.9% over $200k), pre-tax deductions, tax credits, or your specific W-4 elections. Always verify with a CPA or tax software.

01 — Calculator
🧮

Real IRS math, in your browser

The breakdown runs entirely in your browser using official 2024 IRS tax brackets, the standard deduction, and real FICA rates. No server, no guesswork.

02 — Visa logic
🪪

F1/OPT exemption built in

F1 students in nonresident alien status are exempt from Social Security (6.2%) and Medicare (1.45%) under IRS Pub. 519. Most payroll teams miss this. Fin doesn't.

03 — AI chat
💬

Plain-English answers via Claude AI

The "Ask Fin" chat uses Anthropic's Claude API. It's trained to know your visa context and explain things without jargon — not replace a CPA, but help you ask better questions.

04 — Limits
⚠️

What Fin doesn't do

Fin can't file your taxes, give legal advice, or know your exact W-4 elections. For anything complex — two jobs, freelance income, stock vesting — see a CPA who works with international clients.
