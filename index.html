Where this data comes from
Federal tax brackets — IRS Rev. Proc. 2023-34 (2024 tax year)
Standard deduction — IRS Publication 501 ($14,600 single / $29,200 married filing jointly, 2024)
F1/OPT FICA exemption — IRS Publication 519, Chapter 8. F-1 students in nonresident alien status are exempt from Social Security and Medicare taxes.
Social Security wage base — SSA Notice 2023-75 ($168,600 for 2024)
State tax rates — Each state's Department of Revenue (2024 published rates, middle-bracket estimates)

How Fin works

Honest math.
Plain language.

01 — Calculator
🧮

Real IRS math, in your browser

Runs entirely client-side using 2024 IRS tax brackets (Rev. Proc. 2023-34), the standard deduction, and real FICA rates. No server, no guesswork.

02 — Visa logic
🪪

F1/OPT exemption built in

F1 students in nonresident alien status are exempt from Social Security (6.2%) and Medicare (1.45%) per IRS Pub. 519. Most payroll teams miss this.

03 — AI chat
💬

Plain-English answers via Claude AI

The Ask Fin chat uses Anthropic's Claude API with context about your visa status and salary — not to replace a CPA, but to help you ask better questions.

04 — Limits
⚠️

What Fin doesn't do

Fin can't file taxes, give legal advice, or know your W-4 elections. For two jobs, freelance income, or stock vesting — see a CPA who works with international clients.
